EdTech Platforms

Scroll/search for coursers

I love online education and the current landscape of EdTech, so much that I have am pursuing a Masters in Science via an online program. Online education isn’t just innovating on the traditional classroom, it’s a universal and powerful democratization of ideas, intelligence, and experience. The lower cost of production, promoting consumer affordability, will undoubtedly help people from all backgrounds achieve previously unreachable academic success. Below are my reviews of the most popular EdTech platforms and courses I've completed:

Coursera: Courses/Specializations are created/taught by academics from universities across the world. Subjects range from highly technical to liberal arts with a focus on accademia.

Udemy: Courses are created/taught by industry professionals with a highly practical, hands-on approach. Subjects specifically focus on specialized skills.

Udacity: Courses are sponsored by companies primarily in the technology industry. The tech-focused coursers are often advanced but thorough.

EdX: Courses are sponsored by companies and university academics. Subjects range from highly technical to liberal arts with a focus on academia.

Lynda: LinkedIn's learning center, courses are focused on learning specific tools and industry best practices.

Codeacademy: As the name suggests, coursers are focused on computer science and coding. Features an in-browser terminal for practice.

Product Hosted Training: Quality and difficulty varies from product to product. Usually, product hosted training will aim to teach the user basics and real-world applications as quickly as possible
