EdTech Platforms Review

I've found that every online education resource has its own pros/cons and personality. Below are my reviews of the most popular EdTech platforms that I've used:

Coursera: Courses/Specializations are created/taught by academics from universities across the world.
Subjects range from highly technical to liberal arts with a focus on accademia.

Udemy: Courses are created/taught by industry professionals with a highly practical, hands-on approach.
Subjects specifically focus on specialized skills.

Udacity: Courses are sponsored by companies primarily in the technology industry.
The tech-focused coursers are often advanced but thorough.

EdX: Courses are sponsored by companies and university academics.
Subjects range from highly technical to liberal arts with a focus on academia.

LinkedIn Learning: (formerly Lynda) LinkedIn's learning center, courses are focused on learning specific tools and industry best practices.

Codeacademy: As the name suggests, coursers are focused on computer science and coding.
Features an in-browser terminal for practice.

Product Hosted Training: Quality and difficulty varies from product to product.
Usually, product hosted training will aim to teach the user basics and real-world applications as quickly as possible
